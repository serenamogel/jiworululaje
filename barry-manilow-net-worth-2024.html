Early Life and Career Beginnings

Barry Manilow was born on June 17, 1943, in Brooklyn, New York. He developed a passion for music at a young age and started his career as a pianist, arranger, and producer in the 1960s. His breakthrough came in 1974 when he released his hit single “Mandy,” which topped the Billboard Hot 100 chart.

Manilow’s music career is the primary source of his wealth. He has released over 30 studio albums, many of which have been certified multi-platinum. His most successful albums include “Barry Manilow II,” “Even Now,” and “This One’s for You.”
