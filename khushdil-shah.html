Khushdil Shah is one of the talented, hardworking and young Pakistani cricketers who plays the Pakistan Super League since 2017. Shah was born on born 7th February 1995. Currently, he is 25 years old. Primarily, he is an all-rounder batsman but occasionally plays slow and left-arm orthodox bowling.

Career Journey

The cricketer started to play the first-class domestic cricket in Pakistan for Fata Cheetas and FATA team in 2014 and 2015, respectively. Since then he is playing for both of them. Shah was nominated in the Baluchistan’s squad in April 2018 for playing the match in 2018 Pakistan Cup. Consequently, in the same year, he became the leading run-scorer of the team while playing for Federally Administered Tribal Areas in the Quaid-e-Azam One Day Cup of session 2018–19. He scored 463 runs in only seven matches of the tournament.

At the starting of the next year, in March 2019, he was again named in Khyber Pakhtunkhwa’s squad to play for the 2019 Pakistan Cup. On 5th April 2019, Shah made his debut against Punjab. In that tournament, he scored 154 runs and not out.

As concerned as the PSL tournament of player, He played PSL for the first time with Peshawar Zalmi team where he had the position of an Emerging player in the 2017-2018 Pakistan Super League. In 2019, he was picked up by the Multan Sultan franchise in the player auction of 2020.

Not only this, but shah has also represented Pakistan’s squad internationally. Initially, he named in the Pakistan cricket team as an emerging player for the 2018 ACC Emerging Teams Asia Cup, in December 2018. However, he made his first international debut in November 2019 from the national side against Australia. As shah was named by the PCB in October 2019 for Pakistan’s Squad of Twenty20 International. Later on the following month, he was again named in Pakistan’s in the 2019 ACC Emerging Teams Asia Cup squad to play the matches against Bangladesh.
