Bed Bath &amp; Beyond announces another 150 store closures in move to avoid bankruptcy

BED Bath & Beyond has made another move to avoid bankruptcy after crushing financial losses.

Bed Bath & Beyond announced it's closing an additional 150 stores with the hopes it will help turn the company around.

The company continues to reorganize as the retailer found a hedge fund to help dig its way out of $393million in net losses.

The company announced Tuesday it received initial gross proceeds of approximately $225million in the offering and expects to receive an additional $800million of gross proceeds in future installments.

Bed Bath & Beyond said the net proceeds from the transaction will be used immediately to repay outstanding loans.

The move takes bankruptcy off the table for now.

In total, Bed Bath & Beyond reportedly has around $1.2billion in debt.

Bed Bath & Beyond’s money woes is also affecting its subsidiaries: Harmon Face Value and Buybuy Baby.

SHUTTERED LOCATIONS

It comes as the retailer originally announced plans to close at least 120 stores.

The restructuring means low-producing locations are closing in an effort to save money.
