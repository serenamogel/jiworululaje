Biography

S.E. Hinton was born in Tulsa, OK on July 22, 1948. American author of young fiction who penned the classic novel The Outsiders when she was only sixteen years old. She was the 1988 recipient of the American Library Association’s Margaret Edwards Award. Her novels were the inspiration for two Francis Ford Coppola films, The Outsiders and Rumble Fish, both released in 1983. Her novel, The Outsiders, was inspired by her observations of rival gangs at her Oklahoma high school. The novel was published when she was a first-year student at the University of Tulsa.
