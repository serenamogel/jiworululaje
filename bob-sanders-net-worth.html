Bob Sanders’ Career and Net Worth

Bob Sanders is an American retired professional football player with a net worth of $14 million. He played as a strong safety and was drafted in the second round by the Indianapolis Colts in the 2004 NFL Draft. Sanders played for the Colts from 2004 until 2010 and won a Super Bowl Championships in 2007 and two AFC Championships. He signed a five year $37.5 million deal in 2007 and became the highest paid safety in NFL history. Sanders was selected to the Pro-Bowl in 2005 and 2007 and was a First-team All-Pro. He finished his career with over 300 tackles.

In 2011, Sanders signed a one year deal with the San Diego Chargers, but his last four seasons on the NFL ended with him being placed on IR. In 2007, Sanders was named AP NFL Defensive Player of the Year, GMC Defensive Player of the Year, AFC Defensive Player of the Year, and PFWA Defensive Player of the Year. Despite his injuries, Sanders’ successful career as a professional football player has earned him a net worth of $14 million.
